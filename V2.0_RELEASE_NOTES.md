## Summary

Version 2.0 introduces the **Agile V Business Track** -- four new skills that extend Agile V's traceability and verification rigor into business strategy, R&D innovation, go-to-market execution, and operational management. These skills run as a **parallel lifecycle** alongside the existing Engineering Track, with defined integration points for full end-to-end traceability from vision to production.

All new skills follow v1.3/v1.5 context optimization patterns, ensuring minimal context window consumption.

## What's New

### 1. Venture Strategist Skill (`venture-strategist/`)

**Purpose:** Converts vision and market opportunity into traceable business models, product portfolios, and strategic plans.

**Key Features:**
- Vision definition with evidence-backed strategic goals (VIS-XXXX)
- Business Model Canvas with validated assumptions (BM-XXXX)
- Prioritized product portfolio with strategic rationale and market impact (PORT-XXXX)
- Competitive analysis with evidence-backed differentiation claims
- Fundraising/investor tracking with metric traceability (INV-XXXX)
- Introduces **Business Gate 0** (Strategy Approval)

**New Artifacts:**
- `.agile-v/business/VISION.md` (VIS-XXXX strategic goals)
- `.agile-v/business/BUSINESS_MODEL.md` (BM-XXXX canvas components)
- `.agile-v/business/PORTFOLIO.md` (PORT-XXXX prioritized portfolio)
- `.agile-v/business/INVESTOR_LOG.md` (INV-XXXX fundraising tracker)

**Why It Matters:** Defines *what* to build and *why* before the engineering pipeline defines *how*. Portfolio items become product intent for discovery-analyst, closing the gap between business strategy and engineering execution.

**Example Traceability Chain:**
```
VIS-0001 (Market opportunity) → BM-0001 (SaaS revenue model) →
PORT-0001 (Product X) → discovery-analyst (product intent) →
REQ-0001 → ART-0001 → TC-0001 → Verified
```

### 2. R&D Innovator Skill (`rd-innovator/`)

**Purpose:** Manages R&D pipeline from technology scouting through prototype validation to formal product transfer.

**Key Features:**
- Technology Radar with Assess/Trial/Adopt/Hold framework (TECH-XXXX)
- R&D pipeline with stage gates: Explore → Prototype → Validate → Transfer (RDI-XXXX)
- Prototype management with timeboxed validation and pivot-or-proceed decisions (PROTO-XXXX)
- Formal product transfer protocol to engineering pipeline
- IP management with prior art tracking (IPR-XXXX)
- Introduces **Business Gate 1 (R&D)**

**New Artifacts:**
- `.agile-v/business/TECH_RADAR.md` (TECH-XXXX technology assessments)
- `.agile-v/business/RD_PIPELINE.md` (RDI-XXXX innovation initiatives)
- `.agile-v/business/PROTOTYPE_LOG.md` (PROTO-XXXX prototype results)
- `.agile-v/business/IP_REGISTER.md` (IPR-XXXX patent/trade secret tracking)

**Why It Matters:** Separates exploration from production. Prototypes are disposable; validated innovations transfer to engineering as fresh requirements. Technology choices are assessed, trialed, and adopted with evidence -- not adopted by default.

**Example Traceability Chain:**
```
VIS-0001 → PORT-0002 → RDI-0001 (ML recommendation engine) →
TECH-0003 (TensorFlow Lite: Trial) → PROTO-0001 (accuracy test) →
Validated → Transfer Package → OBS-0010 (discovery-analyst) →
REQ-0042 → ART-0042 → TC-0142 → Verified
```

### 3. GTM Executor Skill (`gtm-executor/`)

**Purpose:** Converts product portfolio and business model into actionable go-to-market strategies, launch plans, and growth experiments.

**Key Features:**
- Ideal Customer Profile with validation status (GTM-XXXX)
- Messaging framework where every product claim traces to verified REQs
- Channel strategy with unit economics validation (CHAN-XXXX)
- Phased launch planning coordinated with release-manager (MKT-XXXX)
- Growth experiments with hypothesis-driven testing (GROW-XXXX)
- Sales enablement: battle cards, objection handling, pricing
- Introduces **Business Gate 1 (GTM)**

**New Artifacts:**
- `.agile-v/business/GTM_PLAN.md` (GTM-XXXX strategy components)
- `.agile-v/business/CHANNEL_STRATEGY.md` (CHAN-XXXX with unit economics)
- `.agile-v/business/LAUNCH_PLAN.md` (MKT-XXXX phased launches)
- `.agile-v/business/GROWTH_METRICS.md` (GROW-XXXX experiments + results)

**Why It Matters:** Marketing claims become traceable. Every "our product does X" statement must trace to a verified REQ-XXXX, or be explicitly labeled as projected. Growth experiments follow the same rigor as engineering experiments -- hypothesis, criteria, evidence.

**Example Traceability Chain:**
```
PORT-0001 → GTM-0001 (ICP: mid-market SaaS) → CHAN-0001 (content marketing) →
MKT-0001 (launch campaign) ← RELEASE_PLAN_C1 (engineering deployment) →
GROW-0001 (pricing A/B test) → Results → PORT-0001 updated (venture-strategist)
```

### 4. Business Operations Skill (`business-operations/`)

**Purpose:** Manages financial planning, OKRs, team resources, vendor relationships, and operational compliance.

**Key Features:**
- Financial planning with runway tracking and burn rate alerts (FIN-XXXX)
- OKR cascade from strategic (venture-strategist) → team → individual (OKR-XXXX)
- Resource planning with capacity tracking and utilization monitoring
- Vendor management with risk assessment and SLA tracking (VENDOR-XXXX)
- Operational risk categories with mitigation plans (OPS-XXXX)
- Operational KPIs: burn rate, runway, utilization, OKR progress, vendor SLA compliance
- Introduces **Business Gate 2** (Operational Plan Approval)

**New Artifacts:**
- `.agile-v/business/FINANCIAL_PLAN.md` (FIN-XXXX budgets, P&L, runway)
- `.agile-v/business/OKR.md` (OKR-XXXX objectives with cascading key results)
- `.agile-v/business/VENDOR_REGISTER.md` (VENDOR-XXXX assessments + SLAs)
- `.agile-v/business/OPERATIONS_LOG.md` (OPS-XXXX decisions, risks, events)

**Why It Matters:** Closes the operational gap. Budget lines trace to strategic rationale. Team capacity feeds sprint planning. Vendor risks are assessed before contracts are signed. Runway alerts trigger fundraising actions.

**Example:**
```
VIS-0001 → OKR-0001 (Company: 10K users by Q4) →
OKR-0010 (Engineering: Ship 3 features) → Sprint velocity (product-owner) →
OKR-0020 (Marketing: CAC < $50) → GROW-XXXX validation (gtm-executor) →
FIN-0012 (Marketing budget: $100K) → Budget approval (Business Gate 2)
```

## Workflow Examples

### Workflow 1: New Venture (Full Business + Engineering)

1. **`venture-strategist`** — Vision, business model, product portfolio
2. **Business Gate 0** — Approve strategy
3. **`rd-innovator`** — Technology assessment, prototyping
4. **`gtm-executor`** — Go-to-market strategy, channel planning
5. **Business Gate 1** — Approve R&D portfolio + GTM strategy
6. **`business-operations`** — Financial plan, OKRs, resource allocation
7. **Business Gate 2** — Approve budget + operational plan
8. **`discovery-analyst`** — Portfolio → product intent → candidate REQs
9. **`requirement-architect`** — Formalize REQ-XXXX
10. **`logic-gatekeeper`** — Validate requirements
11. **Human Gate 1** — Approve blueprint
12. **`build-agent-*`** + **`test-designer`** — Parallel synthesis
13. **`red-team-verifier`** — Execute tests
14. **Human Gate 2** — Approve validation
15. **`release-manager`** + **`gtm-executor`** — Coordinated deployment + launch
16. **`observability-planner`** — Monitor production
17. **Feedback** — Production metrics → business decisions → next cycle

### Workflow 2: Product Launch Coordination

1. **`release-manager`** — Engineering deployment plan
2. **`gtm-executor`** — Launch plan aligned to release timeline
3. **`business-operations`** — Budget allocation for launch
4. **Coordinated execution** — Deploy (release-manager) + Launch (gtm-executor)
5. **Monitor** — `observability-planner` (production) + `gtm-executor` (growth)
6. **Iterate** — Growth experiments → portfolio updates → next cycle

### Workflow 3: R&D to Product Pipeline

1. **`venture-strategist`** — Strategic direction for R&D
2. **`rd-innovator`** — Scout → Assess → Prototype → Validate
3. **Business Gate 1 (R&D)** — Approve transfer
4. **`rd-innovator`** — Create Transfer Package
5. **`discovery-analyst`** — Convert to candidate REQs
6. **Continue standard engineering pipeline**

## Compatibility with Existing Skills

All new skills integrate with existing Agile V v1.0-1.5 skills:

- **`venture-strategist`** → feeds → **`discovery-analyst`** (product intent from PORTFOLIO.md)
- **`rd-innovator`** → feeds → **`discovery-analyst`** (validated prototypes), **`requirement-architect`** (tech constraints)
- **`gtm-executor`** ← reads → **`release-manager`** (deployment timing), **`red-team-verifier`** (verified claims)
- **`business-operations`** ← reads → **`agile-v-product-owner`** (velocity), **`compliance-auditor`** (compliance)

**No breaking changes.** Existing projects can adopt Business Track skills incrementally.

## Migration Guide

### For Existing Agile V v1.0-1.5 Projects

**No migration required.** Business Track skills are additive.

**Optional Enhancements:**

1. **Add strategic context:**
   - Run `venture-strategist` to document existing product vision + business model
   - Backfill PORT-XXXX for existing products/features

2. **Formalize R&D:**
   - Run `rd-innovator` to create TECH_RADAR.md from existing technology choices
   - Document prototyping decisions retroactively

3. **Enhance launch process:**
   - Run `gtm-executor` alongside `release-manager` for coordinated launches
   - Track growth experiments formally

4. **Operationalize:**
   - Run `business-operations` to create OKR.md, FINANCIAL_PLAN.md
   - Track vendor relationships in VENDOR_REGISTER.md

### For New Projects

**Recommended workflow:** Start with `venture-strategist` → `rd-innovator` + `gtm-executor` → `business-operations` → Enter engineering pipeline via `discovery-analyst`.

## Breaking Changes

**None.** Version 2.0 is fully backward-compatible with v1.0-1.5.

## Deprecations

**None.**

## Known Limitations

1. **Business Track is optional** — Projects with clear product direction can skip directly to discovery-analyst.
2. **Business Gates are advisory** — Unlike engineering Human Gates which block pipeline progression, Business Gates enforce strategy review before resource commitment.
3. **Financial data sensitivity** — FINANCIAL_PLAN.md and INVESTOR_LOG.md may contain sensitive data. Apply appropriate access controls per your organization's policy.
4. **Market data freshness** — Competitive analysis and market sizing require external data sources. Skills structure the analysis but do not provide market data.
